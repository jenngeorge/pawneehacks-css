History

Though its vales, valleys and ponds are glacier-carved gifts from the last ice age, Prospect Park is as much a marvel of man as of nature. The park, which opened in 1867, was designed by the influential landscape architecture team of Frederick Law Olmsted and Calvert Vaux, who landscaped 585 acres of terrain into an urban retreat reflecting their shared belief that a pastoral playground could have a civilizing effect on a growing metropolis.
